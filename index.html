Project Overview

This website presents a step-by-step calculator to estimate diesel fuel consumption, CO₂ emissions and savings from solar. It also includes local literature-based recommendations for tree species to mitigate remaining emissions in Karachi.

  • Calculator: 3-step interactive tool.
  • Carbon Theory: Simple equations and sources.
  • Trees: Species and sequestration tables drawn from local studies.

Environmental Impact & Mitigation

The Hidden Cost of Our Power Backup

In Karachi, the hum of a diesel generator is a sound we all know too well. For every single liter of diesel consumed, nearly 2.7 kilograms of CO₂ is pumped into our local air, trapping heat and affecting our health.

Turning Karachi’s Heat into Help

Our city receives over five hours of peak sunlight daily. By capturing this energy, we can "turn off" generators during the day, creating an economic shield against rising fuel prices in Pakistan.

Nature’s Own Air Purifiers

Native trees like the Neem and Peepal act as natural vacuum cleaners. They breathe in the CO₂ our machines breathe out, cooling our streets by several degrees through shade.
