Theoretical Insights

Diesel Combustion Dynamics

Analysis of decentralized diesel generators reveals a direct output of $CO_2$ and $PM_{2.5}$, significantly degrading Karachi's local air quality during load shedding.

The Energy Gap Crisis

Systemic grid instability forces reliance on carbon-intensive backup power. This gap is the primary driver for decentralized emission growth.

Photovoltaic Displacement

Solar PV directly offsets diesel fuel consumption. With 5.5 peak sun hours, Karachi's solar potential offers a massive carbon reduction pathway.

Biological Sequestration

Trees act as natural vacuum cleaners for $CO_2$. A mature Neem tree can absorb up to 22kg of carbon annually in urban environments.

Urban Heat Mitigation

Strategic planting counteracts the "Urban Heat Island" effect through evapotranspiration and the creation of shaded micro-climates.

Mathematical Net Zero

Sustainability is reached by balancing aggressive source reduction (Solar) with atmospheric removal (Urban Forestry).
